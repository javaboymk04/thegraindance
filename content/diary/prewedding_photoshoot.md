So I came in prepared with my gears:

All I know is it's overpriced as hell (bought it for Rp. 150.000 saw the original price tag was Rp.20.000 but maybe i need to factor in inflation), it does it's job, and the GN (guide number) is a slide rather than a table which is handy for me.

We promised to meet at [Suropati Park](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FVpwXmYwBG8FBJQv5) at around 10 AM.

You might be asking, "why would you use flash during morning hour?" and the answer to that is: During the shot, it's actually pretty overcast. The sun is still shining but the cloud's are pretty thick, so the overall mood was grey rather than warm yellow. The flash helps making the light on the subject more even.

### Outta the scans
Let's start with the raw straight out of the lab result.
{{< figure

I know this is a goofy picture, but this demonstrates the previous context of flash during daylight. Usually this would result in the subject having their faces darker because the light is coming from background. However, by using flash, I manage to (as best as I can) evenly distribute the light. Which highlights them more rather than highlighting the background (also keep in mind the overcast weather).

This shot also demonstrate the previous point well:
{{< figure
    src="images/prewed/raw/raw_9.jpg"

    class="ma0 center w-90"
>}}

The headroom is mostly filled with the sky, which if I meter the photos by using the light from the sky, it would surely result in a dark subject. The flash helps me to mitigate that.

### Lightroom to the rescue

You might have noticed that the ***"raw"*** picture looks a bit dull, the color isn't as ***"popping"*** as how a lot of the Portra sample pictures would lead you to believe. This is because a lot of those pictures are edited.

Yes, I was once like you, a ***"purist"*** who doesn't believe that film photos should be edited. However, did you know that photos have been edited since photography became mainstream. Even the great **Ansel Adams** edited his picture. But back in the day, they do it manually in the dark room instead of nowadays where we can edit it digitally using Lightroom. Though there's not a lot of leeway when you compare editing a film scanned photos than a digital photos.
