Now these last 3 shots is my *favorite* from the bunch. It's because I took some risk and used the feature that my MX has but I've never used before: **double exposure.**

It's kinda hard to explain how to do it in the MX since it doesn't have a dedicated DP button but basically.
- You take your shot
- You press and hold the film rewind button at the bottom of the camera
- At the same time, you crank the film advance lever to fool the camera into thinking that it has already advanced in to the next frame
- Shoot your next exposure
- Crank it like normal
- Then take a blank frame by putting a lens cap or cupping the lens cap with your palm

## Bottom line
Don't be afraid to take your camera into some indoor gig in the future, I know I'm not going to. However, do be mindful of your wallet.

Because the price of film has exploded into the orbit these past few years, 800 speed film is the most expensive color film available now. The *cheapest* one is Cyberpunk 800T do to it being a respool of those Kodak cinema film. Lomo 800 & Portra 800 are very expensive but they're widely available in Indonesia, unlike Cinestill 800T. I haven't even mentioned the price of development, my local lab charges extra for pushing so make sure to ask your lab if they're willing to push your film and do they charge extra or not!
