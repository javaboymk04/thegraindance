I’ve been listening to this band named Seaside, a local shoegaze indie band based in Jakarta since I was in college. However, I only listen to their single titled “Giggle and Blush.” That song leans more into dreampop feel such as the likes of Slowdive (even Slowdive feels still mixes shoegaze and dreampop) rather than heavy shoegaze like My Bloody Valentine.

Long story short, their band went into hiatus, got themself some new members, almost a decade later they came up with a new album called “Same Old Same New.”

I’ve been listening to their single titled “Foolishly” but the album takes it to whole another level. It’s certainly a mix of both dreampop and shoegaze, but it does lean more heavily on the shoegaze side with twangy and heavy distorted guitar.

Gears

That’s it really, no flash since at that moment I don’t have any flash yet. For the film itself, I prep 2 of them

  • Lomography 800

However, by intentionally putting my camera at ISO 1600 and underexposing the films, I asked my lab to push it by +1 (or in other words, to 1600.)

If you’re a meticulous kind of guy, you might’ve noticed that there’s a “T” in the name of the film, and you could already guess that the “T” stands for “Tungsten.” So going into this I’m expecting a pretty heavy tungsten on the edges of the light. But check out these 2 snaps:

The tungsten actually looks more “diffused.” With my limited knowledge, I’m guessing this is due to the pushing process that alters the look of the tungsten.

Now these last 3 shots is my favorite from the bunch. It’s because I took some risk and used the feature that my MX has but I’ve never used before: double exposure.

It’s kinda hard to explain how to do it in the MX since it doesn’t have a dedicated DP button but basically.

  • You take your shot
  • You press and hold the film rewind button at the bottom of the camera
  • At the same time, you crank the film advance lever to fool the camera into thinking that it has already advanced in to the next frame
  • Shoot your next exposure
  • Crank it like normal
  • Then take a blank frame by putting a lens cap or cupping the lens cap with your palm

Bottom line

Don’t be afraid to take your camera into some indoor gig in the future, I know I’m not going to. However, do be mindful of your wallet.

Because the price of film has exploded into the orbit these past few years, 800 speed film is the most expensive color film available now. The cheapest one is Cyberpunk 800T do to it being a respool of those Kodak cinema film. Lomo 800 & Portra 800 are very expensive but they’re widely available in Indonesia, unlike Cinestill 800T. I haven’t even mentioned the price of development, my local lab charges extra for pushing so make sure to ask your lab if they’re willing to push your film and do they charge extra or not!
